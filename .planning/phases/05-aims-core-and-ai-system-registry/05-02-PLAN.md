    **Document ID:** HH-AIMS-RMA-001
    **YAML frontmatter:** document-id, title, version (v1.0), date, classification (Confidential), author (CISO Agent), reviewer ([CISO, CTO]), approver (CEO Helge Heupel), review-cycle (Annual), next-review, status (Draft), change-summary, references (HH-ISMS-RMA-001, ISO 42001, ISO 23894, AI Policy HH-AIMS-POL-001).

    **9-section structure (from research Pattern 5):**

    1. **Purpose and Scope** — Extends HH-ISMS-RMA-001 for AI-specific context. Satisfies ISO 42001 cl. 6.1.2 and ISO 23894. Applies to ALL AI systems in the AI System Registry. Explicitly states: "This procedure does NOT replace the organization's risk assessment methodology (HH-ISMS-RMA-001). It extends it with AI-specific risk identification sources, an additional impact dimension, and the dual-axis classification framework defined in the AI Policy."

    2. **Normative References** — ISO 42001 cl. 6.1.2, cl. 6.1.3, cl. 8.2; ISO 23894:2023; ISO 42005:2025 (cross-reference); HH-ISMS-RMA-001; HH-AIMS-POL-001 Section 4.

    3. **AI-Specific Risk Sources (ISO 23894)** — 5 categories with 3-5 example risks per category:
       - Category 1: Data and data governance risks (training data bias, data poisoning, data quality degradation, data provenance gaps, data drift)
       - Category 2: Model and algorithm risks (hallucination, prompt injection, model degradation, opacity/explainability, unintended learning)
       - Category 3: System integration risks (API reliability, version incompatibility, context window limitations, tool misuse, cascading failures)
       - Category 4: Operational environment risks (availability dependency on provider, latency, cost overrun, shadow AI, unauthorized use escalation)
       - Category 5: Societal and stakeholder impact risks (individual rights violation, discrimination, misinformation, environmental impact, public trust erosion)

    4. **Dual-Axis Classification** — Reproduce the framework from AI Policy Section 4:
       - Axis 1: EU AI Act Risk Level (Prohibited, High-Risk, Limited-Risk, Minimal-Risk) applied to Product AI
       - Axis 2: SDLC Usage Tier (Tier 1 Assistant, Tier 2 Collaborator, Tier 3 Decision Support)
       - Combined classification matrix: "The higher classification on either axis governs the overall risk treatment requirements"
       - Include a decision tree (text-based flowchart) for classification

    5. **Assessment Process (7 numbered steps)** — Each step with responsible role, input, output, and detailed instructions:
       - Step 1: Establish context (per HH-ISMS-RMA-001 Section 2 Step a) — define AI system scope, stakeholders, jurisdictions
       - Step 2: Identify AI-specific risks — use the 5 ISO 23894 risk source categories; for each AI system, systematically evaluate each category
       - Step 3: Analyze risks — use the existing 5x5 likelihood/impact matrix PLUS the 5th dimension (individual/society impact) for AI risks. The 5th dimension scale: 1=Negligible (no individual impact), 2=Minor (inconvenience to individuals), 3=Moderate (material harm to individuals), 4=Major (significant harm to groups), 5=Severe (systemic societal harm)
       - Step 4: Evaluate against risk acceptance criteria — same risk acceptance thresholds as HH-ISMS-RMA-001; AI risks with individual/society score >= 4 ALWAYS require CEO explicit acceptance regardless of overall risk level
       - Step 5: Select treatment options — Avoid/Modify/Share/Retain per HH-ISMS-RMA-001
       - Step 6: Map to ISO 42001 Annex A controls — identify applicable Annex A controls for each risk; document in risk register entry (applicable-controls field)
       - Step 7: Document in risk register — create/update YAML entry with ai-risk: true tag, all 5 impact dimensions, and ISO 42001 Annex A control references

    6. **Assessment Triggers** — Table with trigger type, description, and required action:
       - Event-driven: new AI system introduction, major model/architecture change, AI incident/near-miss, EU AI Act reclassification, new customer deployment
       - Periodic: annual review cycle for all registered AI systems (aligned with ISMS annual risk review)
       - Regulatory: new EU AI Act implementing acts, new EDPB guidance affecting AI, ISO standard updates

    7. **Roles and Responsibilities** — RACI table for the 7 assessment steps:
       - CISO Agent: R for steps 1-7 (drafts everything)
       - CEO: A for all steps (accountable), approves step 4 risk acceptance
       - Human CISO: C for steps 2-4 (validates in workshop), R for step 4 (challenges ratings)
       - CTO: C for steps 2-3 (validates technical AI risks)
       - AppSec Agent: C for step 2 (identifies technical security risks for AI systems)

    8. **Forms and Templates** — Include as appendices:
       - Appendix A: AI Risk Assessment Form (Markdown template with fields for system-id, assessor, date, risk source category, risk description, likelihood, impact across 5 dimensions, risk level, treatment decision, ISO 42001 Annex A controls, approval)
       - Appendix B: AI System Classification Form (dual-axis classification template)
       - Appendix C: Residual Risk Acceptance Record (CEO sign-off template for AI-specific residual risks)

    9. **Integration with ISMS Risk Assessment** — Explicitly document: single risk register, ai-risk: true tag, same 5x5 matrix extended with 5th dimension, same quarterly review cycle, same risk treatment options, same CEO residual risk acceptance mechanism (Git PR + formal summary).

  <action>
    Create the AI Impact Assessment Procedure following the ISO 42005 structure from 05-RESEARCH.md Pattern 6, with HH-specific content.

    **Document ID:** HH-AIMS-AIA-001
    **YAML frontmatter:** document-id, title, version (v1.0), date, classification (Confidential), author (CISO Agent), reviewer ([CISO, CTO]), approver (CEO Helge Heupel), review-cycle (Annual), next-review, status (Draft), change-summary, references (ISO 42001 cl. 6.1.4, cl. 8.4, ISO 42005:2025, AI Policy HH-AIMS-POL-001).

    **10-section structure:**

    1. **Purpose and Scope** — Satisfies ISO 42001 cl. 6.1.4 and cl. 8.4. Assesses potential consequences for individuals, groups, and societies from AI system deployment. Applies to all AI systems classified as High-Risk or Limited-Risk in the AI System Registry. References ISO 42005:2025 as the structural framework.

    2. **Normative References** — ISO 42001 cl. 6.1.4, cl. 8.4; ISO 42005:2025; Annex A.5.2-A.5.5; EU AI Act Art. 9 (High-Risk), Art. 50 (Limited-Risk); HH-AIMS-POL-001.

    3. **When Assessment is Required** — Table of mandatory triggers (from user decisions):
       - All High-Risk AI systems (EU AI Act Art. 6/Annex III) — full assessment
       - All Limited-Risk AI systems (EU AI Act Art. 50) — focused assessment on transparency and individual impact
       - Any AI system processing special category personal data (GDPR Art. 9)
       - New AI system introduction (before deployment)
       - Significant model change (architecture, retraining, new data)
       - Change in intended use or deployment context
       - Annual periodic review for all AI systems with existing assessments
       - Note: "At Startup, all HH AI systems are Limited-Risk. Full High-Risk assessment process is documented but not yet exercised."

    4. **Impact Dimensions and Thresholds** — Define specific, measurable thresholds per dimension (anti-pattern avoidance: no vague "appropriate" language):
       - Fairness: Disparate impact ratio >= 0.8 (4/5 rule). If < 0.8, mitigation required before deployment.
       - Safety: No autonomous decisions without HITL gate. Any safety-critical output requires dual review.
       - Privacy: No special category data processing without DPIA. Data minimization assessment per output type.
       - Transparency: All AI-generated outputs identifiable as such (EU AI Act Art. 50). Users informed of AI nature.
       - Environmental: Energy consumption per inference documented. Carbon footprint of training not applicable (third-party models).
       - Individual rights: No profiling without explicit consent. No automated decision with legal effect without human review.

    5. **Assessment Process (8 numbered steps, ISO 42005 aligned)** — Each step with responsible role, inputs, outputs:
       - Step 1: Scoping — Define AI system boundary, features, data flows, affected stakeholders, applicable jurisdictions (DE, NL, US)
       - Step 2: Responsibility assignment — Assign assessment team (system owner, CISO Agent draft, CTO technical review, CEO approval)
       - Step 3: Threshold definition — Apply the thresholds from Section 4 to this specific system; document any system-specific threshold adjustments with justification
       - Step 4: Execution — Assess impacts using both quantitative methods (bias testing metrics, privacy analysis) and qualitative methods (stakeholder impact review, rights assessment)
       - Step 5: Analysis — Identify impacts exceeding thresholds; classify severity; propose mitigations with timelines
       - Step 6: Documentation — Document per ISO 42001 Annex A.5.3 requirements: scope, methods, findings, mitigations, residual impacts, assessor details
       - Step 7: Approval — CEO reviews and approves. For High-Risk systems, external review by consultant required. For Limited-Risk, CEO approval sufficient.
       - Step 8: Monitoring — Define impact indicators for ongoing monitoring; set re-assessment triggers; integrate into AI system operational monitoring

    6. **Roles and Responsibilities** — Table mapping 8 steps to roles (AI System Owner, CISO Agent, CTO, CEO, External Consultant)

    7. **Documentation Requirements (per ISO 42001 A.5.3)** — Required sections in every AIA report:
       - System identification (link to AI System Registry entry)
       - Assessment scope and boundary
       - Stakeholder identification
       - Assessment methodology
       - Findings per impact dimension
       - Threshold comparison results
       - Mitigation actions with responsible owners and timelines
       - Residual impact statement
       - Assessor details and qualifications
       - Approval records

    8. **Forms and Templates** — Include as appendices:
       - Appendix A: AI Impact Assessment Form (comprehensive template with all 6 impact dimensions, rating scales 1-5 per dimension, threshold comparison column, mitigation column, timeline column)
       - Appendix B: AIA Report Template (section headers matching Section 7 requirements)
       - Appendix C: Worked Example — CISO Agent (AI-AGENT-001) AIA demonstrating a completed Limited-Risk assessment. Include: scope (compliance artifact generation), stakeholders (CEO, human CISO, auditors), fairness assessment (N/A — no individual profiling), safety (low — outputs reviewed before use), privacy (medium — processes confidential standards), transparency (fully met — Git attribution and YAML metadata), environmental (low — Anthropic API), individual rights (N/A — no external individual interaction), overall assessment: ACCEPTABLE with monitoring.

    9. **Integration with AI Risk Assessment** — Cross-reference to HH-AIMS-RMA-001. Impact assessment findings inform risk register entries (impact dimensions). Risk assessment results inform which systems need impact assessment. Both feed into the AI System Registry (last-risk-assessment, last-impact-assessment dates).

    10. **Startup Phase Simplification** — Explicitly document: "At Startup, all 11 registered AI systems are classified as Limited-Risk under EU AI Act Art. 50. The impact assessment for Limited-Risk systems focuses on transparency obligations and individual/societal impact dimensions. The full High-Risk assessment process (Art. 9-15 conformity assessment) is documented in Sections 4-5 but not yet exercised. When Verity product AI features are finalized, a reclassification assessment will determine if High-Risk assessment is required."
